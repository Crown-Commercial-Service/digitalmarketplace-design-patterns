Help a buyer see all their activities on the Digital Marketplace

This design helps buyers to see a list of all their buying activities on the Digital Marketplace.

How the design works

On their account page, buyers can chose to view their buying activity by framework. Buyers are then shown a list of their published requirements for each framework.

Each requirement is listed by its status, for example published or closed.

Results are listed in chronological order and by:

  • requirement name
  • date
  • status

Why this is useful

This design helps by:

  • prompting buyers to update what’s happening with their procurement, for example by confirming who won the contract
  • giving buyers a place to return to work in progress
  • keeping an audit trail of all the work done

Things to consider

Don’t use this for simple transactions that you don’t need to return to.

It would also be helpful to:

  • provide shared access to a buyer account so team members can work together
  • provide an archive function as the list of past work could get long over time
  • be able to delete unwanted requirements
  • think about about how to present an overview of requirements with multiple frameworks
  • consider the language used and whether it’s more useful to classify by framework name or what’s being bought
