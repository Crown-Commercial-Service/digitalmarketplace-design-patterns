The buyer’s journey

These designs were used on the Digital Marketplace to guide buyers through the buying journey.

The designs helped buyers to buy openly and fairly following procurement regulations.

  • Preparing to buy

    Before going to market, buyers have to complete a number of steps offline. These include deciding what they need, talking to suppliers and getting budget approval.

  • Finding a service or supplier

    Depending on the framework, buyers need to either choose from a list of services or run a competition for suppliers.

    To find services, you may need to help a buyer:

    • get the most relevant search results
    • through the steps in the buying process
    • download a list of suppliers to shortlist

    To find suppliers, you may need to help a buyer:

    • write and publish better requirements
    • see all their activities on the Digital Marketplace

    Buyers may also need help to:

    • cancel or withdraw requirements
    • understand changes to a framework that might affect what they do next

  • Answering supplier questions

    Buyers need to answer any questions that suppliers have and publish their responses.

    Help a buyer to:

    • answer questions while following procurement regulations

  • Shortlisting and evaluation

    Buyers need to shortlist and evaluate their chosen services or suppliers. This process happens offline.

    To help buyers through this process, we’ve written guidance about what to consider when:

    • assessing services
    • shortlisting suppliers
    • evaluating suppliers

  • Awarding a contract

    Once a supplier or service has been chosen, buyers need to publish the winner’s details.

    Help a buyer to:

    • confirm who won the contract
