Help a buyer answer questions while following procurement regulations

This design allows suppliers to ask buyers questions about what they need to buy before applying for the work.

How the design works

Suppliers can ask buyers any questions they have about what the buyer wants. Using this design allows buyers to:

  • clarify to suppliers what’s needed
  • publish the questions for all suppliers to see
  • remove any confidential supplier information before publishing the supplier’s question

Buyers must answer the question publicly and within an agreed time.

Why this is useful

Using this design:

  • creates open and fair competition because the responses are publicly available
  • allows suppliers to clarify what the buyer wants which saves time later
  • means buyers can clarify or add any additional information

Publishing buyers requirements on the Digital Marketplace provides a space for the answers to live. This makes the process open and transparent.

Things to consider

If we were looking at this design again, we’d consider:

  • capturing questions in the Digital Marketplace, rather than by emailing the buyer
  • having shared access to a buyer account to make it easier to share questions around the team
  • including the question in the Digital Marketplace and allowing buyers to edit, rather than expecting them to retype the question

Where else this is used on the Digital Marketplace

The same process is used to answer clarification questions when a framework is opening for supplier applications.
