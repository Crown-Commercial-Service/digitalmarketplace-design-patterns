Help a buyer cancel or withdraw requirements

This design helps buyers if they need to withdraw open requirements or cancel closed requirements.

How the design works

Withdrawing open requirements

Buyers can withdraw open requirements from their account.

Withdrawing a requirement publishes a banner at the top of their requirements page saying:

  • the requirement has been withdrawn
  • suppliers can no longer apply
  • updated requirements may be published in future

Any applicants are emailed and told that their application has been cancelled. Any applications in draft can not be submitted.

Cancelling closed requirements

When the requirement has closed for applications, buyers are asked to confirm the supplier they’ve awarded the contract to.

If the buyer has not awarded a contract they can say if:

  • the requirements have been cancelled
  • there were no suitable suppliers

A banner is published at the top of their requirements page saying what happened with the contract, for example if the contract has been cancelled.

All applicants are emailed to say the buyer is publishing this information on the Digital Marketplace.

Why this is useful

The design makes the buying process transparent by making it clear to suppliers what’s happening with requirements.

Things to consider

By making the process of withdrawing and cancelling part of the buying journey, we help buyers to be more transparent in their feedback to suppliers.

More work could be done to encourage buyers to give more detailed feedback to unsuccessful suppliers.
