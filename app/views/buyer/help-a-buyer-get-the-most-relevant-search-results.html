Help a buyer get the most relevant search results

Help buyers narrow down their search results to make it quick and easy to find the most relevant services.

How the design works

This design helps the user:

  • choose the most relevant high-level category before starting to search
  • search in different ways, for example using keywords, filters or more categories
  • filter their search results down to a manageable number

For the filters, we used the checkboxes component from the GOV.UK design system.

You can read more about how we set up search in the design system.

Why this is useful

By allowing buyers to search for the service that best matches their search criteria this design:

  • makes sure big companies don’t dominate the market as all suppliers are given an equal chance to appear in search results
  • makes buying open and fair by putting the emphasis on finding a service that matches the buyers’ needs and not searching for a supplier already known to the buyer

Things to consider

Don’t use this design if:

  • your list of catalogue entries to search isn’t very long
  • your catalogue entries are distinct and can be refined using one search method

What we did on the Digital Marketplace

The G-Cloud catalogue has over 25,000 services, which means buyers’ searches return a lot of results.

We observed that some buyers were using ‘non-compliant’ behaviour to limit results, for example, using known supplier names.

What we wanted to do

To do this we wanted to change buyers’ behaviour by making them:

  • think about what they needed before they started searching
  • use more than keywords alone to search
  • use filters and categories to refine the number of search results

We also considered:

  • making sure buyers and suppliers were using a shared language to describe services
  • displaying results in a random order to make sure they were fair and never favoured a particular supplier

What we designed

Before buyers can search they have to choose a high-level category.

This gives buyers the option to limit their search results to the general category they’re interested in.

Once on the search page, buyers can use sub-categories and filters to further narrow their search.

Checkboxes allow buyers to apply the filters that are the most relevant to their requirements. Filter options are based on the information suppliers give to describe their services.

We did a lot of research and testing to find a way to describe cloud services that makes sense to both buyers and suppliers.

What we learnt

When we tested and implemented this design we learnt that:

  • including a page before search where buyers choose a category makes buyers think more about what they need
  • building fairness into the system helps buyers meet the procurement regulations
  • buyers expect search to work like Google and we need to provide other options to help them refine their search
  • buyers and suppliers need a shared language, which they don’t have currently

How to improve this

This design could be improved if we:

  • experimented by indexing more information from suppliers’ service descriptions in order to return more relevant search results for buyers - we currently only index the service summary, categories, features and benefits fields
  • explored the benefits of introducing auto-correct or synonyms for search terms
  • created a formal taxonomy of terms that are common to buyers and suppliers
  • spent more time looking at the titles of the filters and making sure they match buyers expectations
