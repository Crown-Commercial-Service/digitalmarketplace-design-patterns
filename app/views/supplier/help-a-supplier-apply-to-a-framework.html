Help a supplier apply to a framework

The design is made up of 3 steps that help a supplier to:

  • make a declaration
  • add their company details
  • add services

How the design works

Use this design if you need to break down distinct application activities that can be carried out by different people.

The design works by:

  • splitting a complex task into smaller sections
  • using a tasklist pattern to give users an idea of what is left to complete
  • centralising company details and putting them at account level so suppliers don’t have to enter the same information twice
  • allowing users to copy answers from previous iterations of the same framework

Components used in the design

The design is based on a mixture of several components in the GOV.UK design system:

  • parts of the tasklist
  • form elements like checkboxes, text fields and radio buttons
  • a version of confirmation banners to reassure suppliers they have completed a section

Why this is useful

This provides a single place for all tasks relating to a framework application.

Helping users add their company information

This section helps suppliers to review and confirm information that, if wrong, would need the support team to change it. That’s information (like a DUNs numbers or company registration number.

It also enables them to change certain ‘low risk’ details themselves like their company address which reduces unnecessary support queries.

Adding a service

This is useful for catalogue services (like G-Cloud) because suppliers are asked to provide the data that is then shown to buyers to help them make a decision. How suppliers define their services becomes the data that buyers use to narrow down requirements.

Things to consider

It might be worth considering that:

  • the tasklist design we used confuses users and the saved searched version performed better in usability testing
  • the supplier declaration is not in plain English and still makes direct reference to the legislation
  • adding services is very time consuming for first-time users
  • suppliers don’t always describe their services accurately which means it can be hard for buyers to find the right services for G-Cloud
