Help a supplier edit or withdraw services

This design allows suppliers to edit or withdraw their live services.

How the design works

In their account pages, suppliers can see all of their services currently live on the Digital Marketplace. Services are listed by framework and then by lot.

Editing services

Each service has an edit button next to it. When clicked, suppliers can edit the details of that service by section:

  • service name
  • about your service
  • service features and benefits
  • documents

Site administrators have a record of all the edits to confirm these are not 'material' changes. Suppliers can’t edit the service attributes section of their service.

Once suppliers have made the changes a banner confirms the changes are live.

Withdrawing services

Suppliers can remove their services one by one using the remove button at the bottom of each service page.

Clicking the ‘Remove service’ button displays a banner confirming:

  • the service will be removed
  • to reinstate the service suppliers will need to contact the Digital Marketplace team

A banner confirms the service has been removed.

The service is still shown in the suppliers’ list but with a ‘removed’, rather than ‘live’, status.

Why this is useful

This design allows suppliers to make changes quickly and easily to their services.

It also means there’s no administrative burden of making the changes for suppliers.

Things to consider

It’d be worth considering:

  • giving the framework authority approval of any changes suppliers want to make to their services
  • if you allow suppliers to remove services also letting them reinstate them, rather than having to email the Digital Marketplace team
  • how better to display the services other than having a list of services, split by lot, on one long page
